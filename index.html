Successful industrialisation requires careful planning and guidance. Governments and businesses must invest in education, energy, and transport systems to support industrial growth. It’s equally important to ensure that industries grow sustainably, protecting both workers’ rights and the environment. Good advice and planning help prevent issues like pollution, overproduction, or uneven economic development.

Another key piece of advice is to strengthen collaboration between the public and private sectors, as well as with international partners. Sharing knowledge, technology, and investment opportunities can help developing nations industrialise faster. Global trade partnerships encourage innovation, open new markets, and promote economic resilience.
